Getting Started: How the Pieces Move

The first thing you need to know in order to play chess is how the pieces move. Each piece moves in a specific way, and it's important to understand these movements in order to play the game effectively.

The Pieces

There are six different types of pieces in chess:

  • King
  • Queen
  • Rook
  • Bishop
  • Knight
  • Pawn

How They Move

Each piece moves in a different way:

  • The king can move one square in any direction.
  • The queen can move any number of squares diagonally, horizontally, or vertically.
  • The rook can move any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
  • The bishop can move any number of squares diagonally.
  • The knight moves in an L-shape, either two squares horizontally and one square vertically, or one square horizontally and two squares vertically.
  • The pawn can move one square forward, but on its first move it can move two squares forward. Pawns capture diagonally, one square forward and to the left or right.

It's important to remember that pieces cannot move through other pieces. If a piece is blocking the way, the piece cannot move to that square.

Now that you understand how the pieces move, you're ready to start playing chess! Check out our other tutorials for more information on the game.
